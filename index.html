Predicting Cryptocurrency

LSTM Models for Forecasting the Value of Bitcoin, Ethereum and Litecoin

Overview

This project aims to build and employ price prediction algorithms for cryptocurrency. At the onset, we understand the volatility of such prices and the range of unpredictable factors that influence the prices of crypto, which is why the predictions that we are generating here are quite short. We are using a Keras LSTM (long-short-term-memory) model with TensorFlow to analyze and predict pricing on Bitcoin, Ethereum and Litecoin. For each currency, the historical Adjusted Close data was used to build into 7 and 30 Day time sequences, which were fed into the model. The data is scaled logarithmically before processing and then reverse scaled for graphing predictions. We tried several optimizations for each model and saved the best performing model for each currency. We are using this model to predict the future price of each currency. All Crypto data is being pulled from CoinApi through Python.

Breakdowns by Currency

Lifetime price, predicted price, and testing results from training.

Bitcoin

Three LSTM models were constructed, the first with the full range of dates, the second and third beginning in 2018, when the price saw a first major increase. In this case, we experimented with using a Bidirectional LSTM, which yielded very high scores for the first model but poorer results for the reduced dataset. As a result, we only used the Bidirectional method for the first model, which we chose for prediction. The first 2 models use a 7 day lookback window for input with 1 LSTM layer and 2 hidden layers. The third model uses 2 LSTM layers and 1 hidden layer. An R2 Score of .92 was achieved with the second model.

Ethereum

Ethereum trained better using a 7 day lookback window. As with Bitcoin, the initial dataset was reduced to focus on the recent 50% of dates for the second two models. The R2 Score was .90 using the third model, which was trained for 300 epochs.

Litecoin

Litecoin used 30 Day Lookback LSTM models with a max R2 Score of .92 using the first model with a learning rate of .0001, trained for 300 epochs. It may be possible to achieve a higher R2 Score with increased epochs of training or a slower learning rate but it is not immediately clear if this will have the effect of greater accuracy in future prediction given the extent that all outside factors have on value change in currencies.

Conclusions

It will take some additional observation and testing to assess the accuracy of the models developed for each currency. Since there are no obvious patterns present in currency price data (as there may be in annual temperature data or any trend with a seasonal pattern), it will be difficult to predict very far ahead. Ideally, these models will be tuned to predict movement more precisely but with this initial deployment, they are at least showing the power of LSTM to learn and train on past patterns.
